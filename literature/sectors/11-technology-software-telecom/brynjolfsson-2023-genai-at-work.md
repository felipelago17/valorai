## Summary

Brynjolfsson, Li, and Raymond conducted a field study at a large firm deploying an AI assistant for customer-support agents. The AI provided real-time suggestions drawn from the firm's top performers. Productivity gains were largest for less experienced and lower-performing workers; top performers gained little. The implication is that AI can accelerate capability diffusion within an organisation, but the aggregate productivity gain depends heavily on the existing skill distribution of the workforce.

## What it lets valorai measure

Calibrates task-level productivity gains for customer-facing knowledge work; supports an experience-curve adjustment (gains taper as workforce average capability rises).

## Caveats / limits

Single-firm, single-context study; customer support is a high-volume, relatively structured task domain that may not generalise to creative or strategic work. NBER working paper — not yet peer-reviewed at time of most citations.
