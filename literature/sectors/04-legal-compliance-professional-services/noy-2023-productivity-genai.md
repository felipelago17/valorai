## Summary

Noy and Zhang conducted a randomised controlled trial in which participants completed professional writing tasks (press releases, short reports, analysis) with or without access to a large language model. AI access substantially reduced time-to-completion and improved externally-rated quality. The performance gap between weaker and stronger writers narrowed — consistent with the Brynjolfsson et al. experience-curve finding. The controlled setting allows cleaner causal inference than field studies.

## What it lets valorai measure

Provides an experimental lower-bound productivity gain estimate for writing-intensive professional services; suitable as a conservative calibration input.

## Caveats / limits

Controlled experimental setting may overstate gains relative to production environments with verification overhead, workflow integration costs, and error-correction requirements. Writing tasks are a subset of professional work.
