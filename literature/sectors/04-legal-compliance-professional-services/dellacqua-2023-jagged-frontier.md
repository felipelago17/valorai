## Summary

Dell'Acqua and colleagues ran a field experiment with Boston Consulting Group consultants, assigning some to use AI assistance on a structured set of tasks. Inside AI's capability boundary, AI-assisted consultants performed markedly better. Outside that boundary — tasks designed to fall beyond current AI competence — AI-assisted consultants performed worse than controls, partly because they applied AI outputs uncritically. The "jagged frontier" metaphor captures the uneven and non-obvious boundary of AI competence.

## What it lets valorai measure

Enables task-composition analysis in professional-services ROI: decompose the work portfolio into inside-frontier and outside-frontier tasks, apply separate value and risk weights.

## Caveats / limits

BCG-sponsored; consultant population is atypical. Task design determines what falls inside/outside the frontier, and that design may not reflect any specific client's task mix.
