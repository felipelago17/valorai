## Summary

The IEA's dedicated report on energy and AI provides the most current authoritative projection of data-centre electricity consumption growth attributable to AI workloads. The headline finding is that global data-centre electricity demand is set to approximately double by 2030 to around 945 TWh — roughly 3% of projected global demand — with AI identified as the primary driver ahead of cloud computing and cryptocurrency. The report connects AI infrastructure growth to grid reliability, the carbon intensity of marginal generation, and the emerging wave of technology-company nuclear and SMR offtake agreements as firms seek low-carbon firm power for data-centre loads.

## What it lets valorai measure

Provides macro-level energy-demand projections for environmental TCO modelling; grounds scenario analysis of electricity-price risk and carbon intensity for any sector with significant AI-compute spend; directly relevant to energy-sector deployments where AI infrastructure costs compound the sector's core operational-energy costs.

## Caveats / limits

IEA updates energy reports annually; verify that this edition (2025) is the most current before citing. Attribution of demand specifically to AI versus broader data-centre growth involves estimation. Optional companion: IEA (2026), "Key Questions on Energy and AI" (April 2026 update).
