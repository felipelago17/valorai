## Summary

MIT NANDA analysed enterprise GenAI deployments and found a stark divide: the large majority of pilots did not produce measurable profit-and-loss impact, while a small proportion of fully integrated systems delivered significant value. Value concentration appears in back-office automation rather than the sales and marketing use cases that attract most budget. Internal builds underperformed relative to vendor and partnership builds. Shadow AI use (personal tools in professional contexts) affects roughly 90% of the workforce, creating governance and data-leakage exposure that organisations largely ignore.

Empirically grounds the 95% pilot-failure base rate; provides the shadow AI governance gap metric; establishes the build-vs-buy differential as a valuation input.
