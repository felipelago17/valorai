## Summary

Survey of 6,000 full-time digital workers (US/UK/Australia, fielded Dec 2025–Jan 2026,
nationally representative by age/gender/income), blended with Glean-platform telemetry and
leader interviews, co-authored with academics. Adoption is near-universal and workers report
saving ~11 hrs/week, yet ~6.4 hrs/week is absorbed by "botsitting" — feeding context,
supervising output, debugging, and cleaning up confident-but-wrong answers. Only 13% of
organisations report performing significantly better. 69% admit "botshitting": shipping work
they have not verified or could not defend.

## What it lets valorai measure

A directional base-rate for the **Verification & Oversight** indirect-cost category. The gap
between self-reported time savings (~11 hrs/wk) and organisational performance gain (13%)
is a clean empirical illustration of ValorAI's core thesis: individual efficiency signals
do not aggregate to organisational value when verification overhead consumes the margin.

## Caveats / limits

Vendor-funded: Glean sells the enterprise "context layer" that botsitting motivates, so the
construct is sponsor-defined and the framing favours the cure they sell. Core figures are
self-reported; independent RCT evidence (METR 2025) shows measured slowdown despite perceived
speedup, so treat magnitudes as soft and the direction as robust. Academic co-authorship is
not peer review. English-speaking advanced economies only. Use triangulated with NANDA (2025)
and Dell'Acqua et al. (2023).
