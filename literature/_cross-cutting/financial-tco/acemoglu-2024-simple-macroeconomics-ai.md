## Summary

Acemoglu applies standard macroeconomic accounting to derive a tractable upper-bound estimate for AI's aggregate productivity effect. Using assumptions about the share of tasks genuinely within AI's competence and the pace of diffusion, he arrives at a 10-year total-factor-productivity uplift of no more than approximately 0.66% — small compared to industry-funded projections. The paper does not argue that AI creates no value — it argues that the automatable task share, diffusion constraints, and complementarity requirements significantly limit the macro impact in the near term.

## What it lets valorai measure

Provides a reality-check ceiling for sector-level and cross-sector value projections; prevents compounding of task-level gains into implausible aggregate figures.

## Caveats / limits

Model depends critically on the assumed task-automation share, which is contested. Does not account for potential general-purpose-technology second-order effects that materialise over longer horizons.
