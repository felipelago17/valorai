# Cross-Cutting Lens: Social & Wellbeing

This lens captures the diffuse but material effects of AI on individual and collective wellbeing: dependency and over-reliance on AI companions or assistants, equity and access effects (AI benefits accruing to higher-income users while costs fall on lower-income communities), mental-health effects of AI-mediated social interaction, and the distributional justice dimension of AI's environmental and labour costs.

**Scoring across sectors:** Wellbeing costs are primarily qualitative and scenario-based. Use structured expert judgement or scenario analysis to estimate probability-weighted adverse outcomes. Flag environmental justice exposure where AI infrastructure costs are borne by communities outside the value chain.
