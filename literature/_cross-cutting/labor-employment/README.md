# Cross-Cutting Lens: Labour & Employment

This lens captures the workforce costs of AI: displacement of workers in automatable task categories, wage-structure shifts, supply-chain labour conditions (annotation, moderation, RLHF micro-work), and the distributional effects of productivity gains that accrue to capital rather than labour. Regulatory exposure arises from supply-chain due-diligence legislation and discrimination claims in AI-assisted hiring.

**Scoring across sectors:** Estimate the share of role hours in automatable task categories using the Acemoglu & Restrepo task taxonomy. Quantify displacement and transition costs. Add supply-chain labour audit and remediation costs where AI relies on outsourced annotation or moderation.
