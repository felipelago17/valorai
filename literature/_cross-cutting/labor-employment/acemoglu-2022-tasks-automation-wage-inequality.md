## Summary

Acemoglu and Restrepo developed a task-based framework distinguishing automation's displacement effect (which reduces demand for certain worker types) from its productivity effect (which raises output). Applying this framework to US data, they link the rise of automation to a significant share of the increase in wage inequality observed over recent decades. For valorai, the framework provides a principled way to estimate which worker categories face displacement risk from AI automation and to translate that risk into a financial cost in the TCO model.

## What it lets valorai measure

Enables task-based workforce-displacement cost estimation: categorise tasks by automation susceptibility, apply displacement probability, estimate retraining and transition cost per worker affected.

## Caveats / limits

Based on historical automation (robots, earlier-generation software); generative AI may have different task-displacement patterns affecting knowledge workers differently from the manufacturing workers who dominate the historical data.
