## Summary

Short commentary in *Joule* constructing an illustrative electricity demand scenario for
large-scale generative-AI deployment. de Vries estimates that if every Google search
were served using ChatGPT-level compute, the resulting electricity demand could reach
roughly 29 TWh per year — comparable to Ireland's annual electricity consumption. The
paper draws attention to the scale mismatch between current AI energy cost and the
infrastructure required for widespread deployment. It also notes the water consumed
by data-centre cooling as a compounding environmental cost.

## What it lets valorai measure

A widely cited order-of-magnitude anchor for AI electricity demand that can be used
alongside per-inference measurements (Luccioni et al. 2024) and realised demand
trajectory data (IEA 2025) to triangulate energy cost estimates. Establishes the
"small-country scale" framing that contextualises AI energy use for non-technical
decision-makers.

## Caveats / limits

Commentary/letter (2 pp.), not a full empirical study — it presents *illustrative
projections* from a back-of-envelope scenario, not measured data. The 29 TWh figure
applies a specific assumption about query volume and energy-per-query that should be
checked against current model generations before citing. Tools and efficiencies have
changed substantially since October 2023. Triangulate with Luccioni et al. (2024) for
measured per-inference emissions and IEA *Energy and AI* (2025) for verified
data-centre demand growth; do not let this commentary stand alone in an evidence chain.
