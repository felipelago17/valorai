## Summary

Peer-reviewed research article in *Patterns* (Cell Press, open access) quantifying the carbon
and water footprints of data centres and modelling the implications for artificial intelligence
growth. Estimates AI's 2025 carbon footprint at 32.6–79.7 Mt CO₂ equivalent and water
footprint at 312.5–764.6 billion litres. The ranges are deliberately wide: operators do not
disclose AI-specific energy and water consumption, so estimates must be derived from inference
about AI's share of total data-centre loads. Same author as the 2023 *Joule* commentary on
AI electricity demand.

## Caveats / limits

Estimates are *disclosure-limited*: the wide ranges (factor ~2.4× for carbon, ~2.4× for water)
directly reflect the absence of operator-reported AI-specific energy and water data. Do not
cite the mid-points as measured fact — the ranges are the result. The methodology is an
inference from data-centre aggregate loads, not AI-specific metering. The 2025 scope means
results will shift as the AI workload mix and data-centre energy mix evolve. Always present as
ranges; never as point estimates.
