# Cross-Cutting Lens: Cognitive & Skills Erosion

This lens captures the degradation of human cognitive capability and tacit organisational knowledge that accompanies sustained AI use: deskilling (atrophy of skills no longer practised), automation bias (uncritical acceptance of AI outputs), cognitive offloading (delegation of reasoning to AI reducing independent mental engagement), and the associated trust-and-accountability risks when review quality degrades.

**Scoring across sectors:** Estimate the share of high-judgement tasks delegated to AI. Apply a skills-erosion discount to long-run productivity projections. Provision for deskilling recovery costs and automation-bias incident exposure.
