## Summary

Lee and colleagues surveyed 319 knowledge workers about their AI tool use and self-reported cognitive engagement (936 AI-assisted work examples). Workers who expressed higher trust in AI outputs reported exerting less independent critical effort — a pattern consistent with cognitive offloading. The shift is from producing to verifying: rather than reasoning through a problem, workers check whether the AI's answer seems plausible. This is a subtler and more pervasive cost than the automation-bias incident, as it operates continuously rather than only at the moment of a specific error.

## What it lets valorai measure

Grounds the skills-erosion discount applied to long-run productivity projections; provides a mechanism-level explanation for why deskilling accrues even in the absence of obvious automation-bias incidents.

## Caveats / limits

Self-reported; susceptible to social-desirability bias. Survey methodology cannot establish causation — workers who were already less critically engaged may preferentially adopt AI tools.
