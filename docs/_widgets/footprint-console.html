Honesty. No measured global total for AI water exists. On-site water is power × an operator-reported ratio; indirect water is power × a regional grid estimate. Per-response energy is the weakest input — vendors rarely publish it and figures vary by model size, hardware and batching. Treat outputs as order-of-magnitude; the water totals especially can be off by 2–3×. This calculator refuses to print a number without its provenance tag — that is the point.
How this is built & sources

Live grid: NESO Carbon Intensity API — api.carbonintensity.org.uk (keyless). Carbon intensity in gCO₂/kWh; generation mix as fuel-type percentages, half-hourly, UTC.
Water benchmarks: operators' own reported WUE — AWS 0.12, Microsoft 0.27; US fleet and industry averages from public sustainability reporting and LBNL's 2023 US data-centre estimate (indirect ≈4.5 L/kWh). On-site water is derived from power, not measured.
Per-response energy: a user-set modelled input; published LLM-inference estimates vary by more than an order of magnitude, so it is exposed as an editable field rather than asserted.
